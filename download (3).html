      Learn

      Learn to develop a framework of success through explaining careers lessons, Lambda X and endorsements.

      Lambda School is investing in you and your success is our success. We want you to succeed and have an amazing job offer (or a few) at the end of the program.

      Overview

      We are constantly learning and changing our curriculum to be sure you have the skills that employers are looking for. The technical skills are actually the easier part to teach and learn. They are very concrete and you either know them or you don’t. It is the career skills and people “soft” skills that can be the more difficult to teach. Soft skills like good interpersonal communication, self-awareness and emotional intelligence are part of who a person is, but they can still be taught and improved upon. Career skill expectations are always changing and an interview at a tech company is going to be very different than a minimum wage job interview. At Lambda school we are going to focus on “soft” skills and career skills from the first day.

      Careers Lessons

      Each week there is a careers lesson presented. These lessons cover a wide variety of topics including job search skills, presentation skills, social media presence and financial planning to name just a few. These are the necessary skills to help you land a great job. The career lessons also have a required assignment component and you are expected to complete these along with your technical assignments.

      Lambda X

      Lambda X is comprised of Build Weeks, Labs and Lambda X.

      Our program helps you reach your full potential by creating meaningful products using all the skills you will learn at Lambda. Lambda X gives students the real-world knowledge, training, an assigned team, and product specifications to simulate what their life will be like as a developer or data scientist in a company/organization. In Lambda X you will receive targeted support and lessons on teamwork.

      Build Weeks

      The fourth sprint in every unit is a build week. In a build week you spend a week on building a real-world based project based on what you have learned. You will come together with students from all the other tracks and with varying levels of experience. You will be assigned a role and tasks based on your track and experience. By the last day of build week you will have a MVP (minimally viable product) to share. You can even apply and be chosen to present your teams product to the entire school.

      Labs

      You will have 8 sprints in Labs that occurs between your core curriculum and additional curriculum. In labs you will have longer to work on a project and receive targeted lessons on teamwork. This product will be one of the core pieces of your portfolio. The experience working long term with a diverse team will give you the experience you need to be successful at your first job.

      Lambda X

      Lambda X is 40% weekly guided job search activities designed to help you do the right things every week that lead to a job. Includes job sourcing, applications, interview practice, technical reviews, and more. The other 60% is portfolio building where you continue building on new or existing Labs projects to add to your portfolio and stay sharp. Students who don’t continue coding after graduation are only 20% as likely to get a job as those who continue to code every day.

      Lambda Endorsement

      The Lambda Endorsement is the definitive signal you’re ready to crush it on the job. Endorsed Lambda students didn’t just make it to the end of an online course, they showed up day in and day out, learning and proving mastery across a wide range of technical, career, and collaborative skills. Lambda Endorsement is our guarantee to you as the student that you’re ready to land a great job in the industry, and our guarantee to hiring managers that you’ve nailed every expectation they could have for you. In addition to proving to yourself and to hiring managers that you’ve graduated from Lambda School, here are a few more benefits to getting endorsed.

      • Lifetime access to Lambda X for portfolio building and job search support
      • Priority for flexible PM roles during job search
      • Weekly sessions with local industry mentors
      • Guaranteed interviews with matched hiring partners
      • Featured profiles on upcoming hiring company portal
      • Offer negotiation support (average of 17% additional compensation)
      • Lifetime access to updated track curriculum, career workshops, and coach support

      Learn to understand the basics of the career and professional development curriculum

      Overview

      While you are at Lambda School to build new technical skills, you are also here to change or grow in your career. Careers at Lambda School is not just about helping Students get their next job, but also about building the professional skills that they will be able to use throughout their career. We build Careers in to every step of the student journey with Lambda School so that students are ready to launch their career as soon as possible after completing the curriculum!

      Follow Along

      Career Lessons

      Students will start career lessons early in their Lambda School coursework. During their course track, we will cover professional development and on-the-job success so they can put these concepts into practice as they move through the curriculum. These lessons cover topics like team dynamics, how to deliver/receive feedback professionally, creating a strong professional brand, and building a network of peers both within and external to Lambda School.

      Students will have dedicated time to work on their resumes, LinkedIn profiles, artifacts, interview abilities, and any other tools or skills that they will need to be successful in their job search.

      Lambda X is the final unit of the curriculum, and this will be focused on brushing up on a Student’s technical skills and polishing all of their job search tools. Students will work closely with members of the Careers and Hiring Solutions teams, and will have access to mentors who can help them find an awesome job in their field!

      At any point in the program, students have access to Career Coaches through daily office hours, where they can get 1:1 support on any career or professional topic they are seeking insight on.

      Core Track Career Lessons

      During your core track you will receive a careers lessons once per sprint. The assignment for this module of Lambda Launch is actually our first careers assignment and you are expected to complete it to be prepared for your following careers lessons.

      How to Work With a Career Coach

      Starting day one at Lambda, you can start working with our Career Coach team. Career Coaches help you define your career plan, create your target list of companies, come up with a job search strategy, create your application materials, develop a networking plan, practice for interviews, learn how to follow-up after interviews, negotiate your salary, succeed on the job, and more.

      How to get help from a Career Coach:

      • Stop by Career Office Hours. These happen Sunday - Friday and the schedule is both posted daily in the #career_help channel and in the calendar, which is also pinned to the top of the channel.
      • Submit a /frontdesk ticket. This will get a direct response from a career coach within 24 business hours. Please see our FAQ for frequently asked questions like Lambda’s phone number/address, how to get a transcript for job applications, etc.

      Learn to articulate how Lambda supports them in their job search and the importance of becoming endorsed

      Overview

      Lambda Endorsement is our signal to students and employers that a student is career ready. Endorsement signifies that a student has not only met/exceeded all technical expectations of the Lambda School curriculum, but also that they have demonstrated a strong ability to work with peers and drive their job search and career development.

      At Lambda School Endorsement = Graduation… Graduation = Endorsement.

      Follow Along

      Steps to Endorsement

      To keep Students on track towards full endorsement, there are multiple checkpoints and milestones during each unit. To receive an endorsement, all Students will need to meet/complete the following technical and professional endorsement categories

      Technical

      1) Complete each Sprint Challenge with a 2+ on each objective 2) Complete each Build Week assessment with a 2+ on each objective 3) Complete a Technical Endorsement Review: Technical Endorsement Review

      Professional

      Once a Student completes the Lambda School coursework, a member of the Careers team will review and sign off on the following:

      • Resume
      • LinkedIn
      • GitHub (Web, iOS, AND, DS tracks only)
      • Portfolio and artifacts for the Student’s track
      • 1:1 behavioral interview

      Students can find detailed expectations and requirements, along with checklists to complete all of the above: Professional Endorsement Review

      Each of the above endorsement categories will be evaluated as a Student moves through Lambda’s curriculum. If a Student needs additional time to work on any of the endorsement categories, we’ll work with them during Lambda X to help them get Lambda Endorsed and career-ready.

      Maintaining Endorsement

      Once endorsed, it is expected that a student will;

      • Maintain communication with career coaches, updating them on upcoming interviews and offers
      • Request a coach look over their resume, LinkedIn, portfolio or other artifacts when making changes or updates to gain another set of eyes to catch formatting or grammatical errors
      • Communicate with hiring partners who reach out, ensuring they always receive a reply or update to any communications within 24 hours
      • Uphold scheduled interviews, and professionally communicate if they need to cancel or rearrange the interview to a different time
      • Continuously work on new projects or maintain old projects to stay fresh on technical abilities.

      Benefits of Endorsement

      Working toward your Lambda School Endorsement opens a variety of doors simply by you continually leveling up your skills to meet the high bar required for endorsement. Additionally, getting endorsed gives you the following exclusive benefits:

      • Sessions with local industry mentors
      • Interviews with matched hiring partners
      • Featured profiles on upcoming hiring company portal
      • Offer negotiation support (avg 17% additional comp)
      • Lifetime access to updated track curriculum, career workshops, and coach support

      Hiring Solutions

      There is a whole team at Lambda whose focus is on getting students hired. This team reaches out to companies to tell them about Lambda School and why they should hire you. This team can help you reach out to companies, just like they do, to assist you in making your job search more effective. This team has talked to hundreds of companies and are building relationships with hiring managers. If they talk to a company that is looking for someone like you (and you are endorsed) they will introduce you.

      Learn to understand that the Lambda curriculum has a singular focus on career readiness.

      Overview

      There is a team of curriculum developers at Lambda School that work on the training kit content, guided project lesson plans, module projects, sprint projects and assessments. With as quickly as technical subjects change, they are tasked with updating content continuously. The curriculum developer works in conjunction with a subject matter expert and instructors to ensure that the curriculum is techically correct and teaches the necessary concepts.

      Start with the End in Mind

      At Lambda, we have the unique opportunity to work closely with hiring partners and industry experts when designing every component of our curriculum.

      The very first step when starting to design curriculum, is to work with these partners to develop the learning objectives. We get to ask ourselves about the specific skills a student must have to be hired in their chosen track. What must they understand? What attitudes and dispositions must they possess?

      Once we have this large list of objectives, we can work backwards from there to design our modules, module projects, sprints, sprint challenges, units, and build week projects. This ensures that we engineer every moment of a student’s Lambda journey with the most important outcome in mind: getting the student a job!
